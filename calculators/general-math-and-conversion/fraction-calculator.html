How to Use the Fraction Calculator

Our online fraction calculator makes it easy to perform mathematical operations with fractions. Simply enter the numerator and denominator for each fraction, select your desired operation (add, subtract, multiply, or divide), and get instant results with detailed step-by-step solutions.

Fraction Operations Explained

Adding Fractions

To add fractions, find the least common denominator (LCD), convert both fractions to equivalent fractions with the same denominator, then add the numerators while keeping the denominator the same.

Subtracting Fractions

When you subtract fractions, follow the same process as addition but subtract the numerators instead of adding them.

Multiplying Fractions

To multiply fractions, simply multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together. This is the easiest fraction operation.

Dividing Fractions

To divide fractions, multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal (inverted form) of the second fraction.

Fraction Simplification

Our calculator automatically simplifies fractions to their lowest terms by finding the greatest common divisor (GCD) of the numerator and denominator and dividing both by this value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you add fractions with different denominators?

To add fractions with different denominators, first find the least common denominator (LCD). Convert both fractions to equivalent fractions using the LCD, then add the numerators while keeping the denominator the same.

What is the easiest way to multiply fractions?

Multiplying fractions is straightforward: multiply the numerators together to get the new numerator, and multiply the denominators together to get the new denominator. Then simplify if possible.

How do you convert a fraction to a decimal?

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator. Our calculator automatically shows the decimal equivalent of your fraction result.

When do you need to find a common denominator?

You need to find a common denominator when adding or subtracting fractions. For multiplication and division, you don't need a common denominator.

What does it mean to simplify a fraction?

Simplifying a fraction means reducing it to its lowest terms by dividing both the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD).

Can this calculator handle improper fractions?

Yes, our fraction calculator works with proper fractions, improper fractions, and mixed numbers. It will automatically handle all types of fraction inputs.
