Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a percentage of a number?

To calculate a percentage of a number, multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For example, 25% of 80 = (25 × 80) ÷ 100 = 20. Use our "What is X% of Y?" calculator for instant results.

How do I find what percentage one number is of another?

Divide the first number by the second number and multiply by 100. For example, to find what percentage 15 is of 60: (15 ÷ 60) × 100 = 25%. Our "X is what % of Y?" calculator makes this easy.

How do I calculate percentage increase or decrease?

Subtract the original value from the new value, divide by the original value, and multiply by 100. For a price increase from $100 to $120: ((120 - 100) ÷ 100) × 100 = 20% increase.

What's the difference between percentage and percentage points?

Percentage points refer to the arithmetic difference between two percentages. If unemployment rises from 5% to 7%, that's a 2 percentage point increase, but a 40% relative increase.

How do I calculate compound percentage changes?

For multiple percentage changes, apply each change sequentially. A 10% increase followed by a 20% increase on $100: $100 × 1.10 × 1.20 = $132 (32% total increase).

Can I use this calculator for negative percentages?

Yes, our percentage calculator handles negative values and percentages. Enter negative numbers for decreases or use our percentage change calculator to see increases and decreases automatically.
