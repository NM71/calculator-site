About BMR

What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to maintain basic physiological functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production while at rest.

Calculation Methods

Harris-Benedict is widely used, Mifflin-St Jeor is considered most accurate for general population, and Katch-McArdle accounts for body composition when body fat % is known.

Activity Factors

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is calculated by multiplying BMR by activity factors: Sedentary (1.2), Light (1.375), Moderate (1.55), High (1.725), Very High (1.9).

Practical Use

Use BMR and TDEE for weight management: eat below TDEE to lose weight, above to gain weight, or match TDEE to maintain current weight. Consult professionals for personalized advice.
